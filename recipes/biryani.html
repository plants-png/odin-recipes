Biryani

Description

A photo of chicken biryani. Biryani is a fragrant, flavourful mixed rice dish that combines meat, fish or vegetables with long-grain Basmaati rice and a rich blend of warm spices like saffron, cardamom and cinnamon.

Ingredients

  • 750 g chicken thighs

    • Marinade

  • 150 ml yoghurt (plain)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 6 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 2 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
  • 1/8 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp coriander
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 1 3/4 tsp salt

    • Par boiled rice

  • 2 tbsp salt
  • 10 whole cloves
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 1 star anise
  • 6 green cardamon pods
  • 450 g uncooked basmati rice

    • Crispy onions

  • 2 medium onions (halved and finely sliced)
  • 250 ml oil (for frying)

    • Saffron

  • 1 tsp saffron threads
  • 2 tbsp warm water

    • Biryani

  • 1 cup coriander / cilantro (chopped)
  • 60 g ghee (melted)

    • Garnish

  • Crispy onions (above)
  • Chopped coriander / cilantro
  • Yoghurt

Steps

  1. Mix Marinade in a large pot (about 26cm diametre). Add chicken and coat well. Marinate for 20 minutes to overnight.
  2. Par Boil the Rice
    1. Bring 3 litres of water to the boil, then add the salt and spices. Add the rice, bring back to the boil, then cook for 4 minutes, or until the rice is just cooked but still slightly firm in the centre. Drain immediately and set aside. (See Note 10 regarding leaving the whole spices in.)
  3. Make the Crispy Onions
    1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Cook the onion in batches for 3–4 minutes, until golden brown. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Repeat with the remaining onion.
  4. Prepare the Saffron
    1. Place the saffron in a bowl and leave it for at least 10 minutes.
  5. Cook the Biryani
    1. Place the pot containing the chicken onto the stove over medium heat.
    2. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
    3. Remove the lid and cook for another 5 minutes, turning the chicken twice.
    4. Remove from the heat.
    5. Turn the chicken so the skin side is facing down, covering most of the base of the pot.
    6. Scatter over half of the crispy onion, then half of the coriander.
    7. Top with all of the rice.
    8. Gently pat down and flatten the surface.
    9. Drizzle the saffron over the rice in a random pattern, then drizzle over the ghee.
    10. Place the lid on and return the pot to the stove over medium heat.
    11. As soon as you see steam, reduce the heat to low and cook for 25 minutes.
    12. Remove from the stove and let it rest, with the lid on, for 10 minutes.
  6. Serve
    1. Aim to serve so you get patches of yellow rice, white rice, curry-stained rice, and chicken rather than mixing everything together.
    2. Use a large spoon, dig deep into the pot, and try to scoop up as much as possible in each serving.
    3. Transfer to a serving bowl or platter.
    4. Garnish with the remaining crispy onion and coriander, and serve with yoghurt on the side.
